ABOUT BIRD WILDLIFE ADVOCACY

The Bird Advocacy Foundation is a not-for-profit organization dedicated to protecting birds and fostering cooperative relationships between humans and avian wildlife. Operating across Australia and New Zealand, their mission is to create harmony between humans and bird wildlife. They acknowledge and respect the Traditional Owners of the land in Australia, including past, present, and emerging custodians. The foundation conducts projects related to education, advocacy, and research, including the Little Chicks Education Program. Their accomplishments include collaborating with the Rural City of Murray Bridge to implement an innovative and wildlife-friendly approach to managing Little Corellas, championing the welfare of native wild birds and bats, and promoting ethical wildlife control practices. Through these endeavors, the Bird Advocacy Foundation contributes significantly to the well-being of our feathered friends and the preservation of their habitats.

OUR VISION

To create harmony between humans and bird wildlife.
The Bird Advocacy Foundation vision can be achieved by:
1.Protecting Birds from being shot, poisoned or trapped.
2.To lobby all levels of government and the productive horticulture industry, to commit to, and to support:
•Creation of alternate feeding source sanctuaries around all productive horticulture regions.
•Restoration of diverse native flora in areas impacted by human activity thereby providing natural and alternative food sources and offering a refuge for native birds.

OUR MISSION

“To advocate for the protection, ethical treatment and preservation of bird wildlife, whilst promoting better commercial and social practices and improve outcomes for the Australian community when considering human and bird interactions.” The Bird Advocacy Foundation aim is to shine the light on unethical growers and affiliated industries within the horticultural sector or public/ private industry sectors who engage in culling, poisoning or other non-humane methods of wildlife control.
